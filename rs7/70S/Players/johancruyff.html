    Johan Cruyff

    Short bio

    Hendrik Johannes Cruijff, commonly known as Johan Cruyff, was a Dutch professional footballer and manager born on April 25, 1947, and he passed away on March 24, 2016. Recognized as one of the all-time greatest players and the finest Dutch footballer in history, he clinched the Ballon d'Or three times-1971, 1973, and 1974. Cruyff embraced the total football philosophy, pioneered by Rinus Michels, both as a player and later as a manager. Due to the profound influence of his playing style and coaching concepts, he is widely acknowledged as one of the most impactful figures in contemporary football. His managerial accomplishments further solidify his status as one of the greatest football managers of all time.
