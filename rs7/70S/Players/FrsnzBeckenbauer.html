    Franz Beckenbauer

    Short bio

    Franz Anton Beckenbauer (born September 11, 1945) is a retired German professional football player and manager. Known by the nickname "Der Kaiser" (lit. 'The Emperor'), he is widely esteemed as one of the premier players in the history of the sport. Initially a versatile midfielder, Beckenbauer gained prominence as a central defender. He is often credited with pioneering the role of the modern sweeper (libero). Achieving success both at the club and international levels, he stands among the select group of nine players who have secured victories in the FIFA World Cup, the European Champions Cup, and the Ballon d'Or.
