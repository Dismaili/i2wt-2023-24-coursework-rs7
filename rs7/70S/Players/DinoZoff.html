    Dino Zoff

    Short bio

    Dino Zoff OMRI (born February 28, 1942) is a retired Italian professional footballer renowned for his role as a goalkeeper. He holds the record as the oldest World Cup winner, having captained the Italy national team to victory in the 1982 tournament at the age of 40 years, 4 months, and 13 days. Zoff, who received the best goalkeeper award and was named to the tournament's team for his outstanding performances, had earlier achieved similar honors after winning the 1968 European Championship on home soil. Notably, he is the sole Italian player to have triumphed in both the World Cup and the European Championship. In addition to his international success, Zoff enjoyed significant achievements with Juventus, securing six Serie A titles, two Coppa Italia titles, and a UEFA Cup. His club accomplishments also included reaching two European Champions' Cup finals in the 1972-73 and 1982-83 seasons, along with finishing as the runner-up in the 1973 Intercontinental Cup final.
