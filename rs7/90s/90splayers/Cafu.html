    Cafu

    Short bio

    Cafu, born on June 7, 1970, in Itaquaquecetuba, São Paulo, is a Brazilian football legend renowned for his exceptional career as a right-back. Starting with São Paulo FC, he moved to AS Roma, winning the Serie A title in 2001. Cafu captained Brazil to victory in the 2002 World Cup, showcasing his leadership and skill. His success extended to AC Milan, where he secured the Serie A and UEFA Champions League in 2007. Cafu's enduring legacy includes being the first to play in three consecutive World Cup finals. Post-retirement, he remains involved in philanthropy and football ambassadorial roles.
