    Lothar Matthäus

    Short bio

    Lothar Matthäus, born on March 21, 1961, in Erlangen, Germany, is a retired German football legend renowned for his versatility and leadership. Matthäus enjoyed a stellar career, notably with Bayern Munich and Inter Milan, earning individual honors such as the FIFA World Player of the Year in 1991 and the Ballon d'Or in 1990. He captained West Germany to victory in the 1990 FIFA World Cup, showcasing his influence on the international stage. Post-retirement, Matthäus transitioned into coaching and punditry, leaving an indelible mark on the sport. His skill, adaptability, and achievements make him a revered figure in football history.
