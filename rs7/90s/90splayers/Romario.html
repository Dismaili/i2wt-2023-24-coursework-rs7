    Romario

    Short bio

    Romário de Souza Faria, born on January 29, 1966, in Rio de Janeiro, Brazil, is a retired football legend celebrated for his prolific goal-scoring prowess. Beginning his career with Vasco da Gama, Romário achieved success with clubs like PSV Eindhoven and Barcelona, winning domestic and international titles. His standout moment came in the 1994 FIFA World Cup, where he led Brazil to victory, earning the Golden Ball as the tournament's best player. Romário's post-retirement career includes a foray into politics, showcasing his enduring impact on and off the football pitch.
