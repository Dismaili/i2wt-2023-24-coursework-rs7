    Toni Schumacher

    Short bio

    Toni Schumacher, born on November 6, 1954, in Düren, West Germany (now Germany), is a retired German football goalkeeper. Schumacher gained fame for his remarkable goalkeeping skills and played a crucial role in Germany's success during the 1980s.

    Schumacher is best known for his performances in international competitions, including the FIFA World Cup, where he contributed significantly to Germany's achievements. His legacy extends beyond the pitch as he remains a respected figure in football.

    Awards

    Individual

    • 1984: German Footballer of the Year
    • 1985: FIFA World Cup Best Young Player
    • 1986: UEFA European Championship Team of the Tournament
    • 1987: UEFA Cup Winner's Cup Final Man of the Match
    • 2005: German Football Ambassador - Special Award
    • 2010: Düren's Sportsman of the Century
    • 2019: FIFA 100 Greatest Living Players

    Team

    • 1981: Bundesliga - 1. FC Köln
    • 1983: DFB-Pokal - 1. FC Köln
    • 1986: Bundesliga - 1. FC Köln
    • 1988: DFB-Pokal - 1. FC Köln
    • 1990: Bundesliga - Bayern Munich

    National Team

    • 1980: UEFA European Championship - West Germany (Runner-up)
    • 1982: FIFA World Cup - West Germany (Runner-up)
    • 1986: FIFA World Cup - West Germany (Runner-up)

    Clubs

    • 1972–1987: 1. FC Köln (West Germany)
    • 1987–1987: Atalanta (Italy)
    • 1987–1988: 1. FC Köln (West Germany)
    • 1988–1996: Bayern Munich (Germany)
